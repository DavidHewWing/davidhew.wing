How I improve myself.

"Good, better, best. Never let it rest. Until your good is better and your better is best." ~ Tim Duncan

Ultimate Frisbee

My first competitive season of Ultimate Frisbee was playing for the Gee-Gees in 2017. In many sports players often bully, or harm their opponent to gain an advantage. However, in Ultimate this is never the case. The game relies on the spirit of sportsmanship to played. Embracing the fact that you don't need to bring someone down in order to succeed.

As a beginner in Ultimate, I was at a disadvantage and it was up to me to improve as fast as I could. I couldn't wait for someone to approach me to get better. I needed to ask the stupid questions and to put in the extra work.

Last year I'd hesitate to say I was great or even good at Ultimate, today I can say with confidence that I am a great Ultimate player. More importantly, Ultimate Frisbee enabled me to become a better person. I tapped into confidence I never had before, gained some extra grit and became a better individual in general. In the future, I will continue to get better and hopefully become an instrumental piece at CUMC for Crank Ultimate
